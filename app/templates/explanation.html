What is procrastination?



Procrastination is the act of delaying or postponing something.

To find the solution to procrastination, we must first look for the root cause.


The Temporal Motivation Theory is a theory developed by Piers Steel and Cornelius J. König which encapsulates the 4 main root causes of procrastination.
  • Self-efficacy
  • Value of task
  • Distractibility
  • Delay of task

Self-efficacy: People with more self-efficacy believe they can perform the task, making them have greater expectations of success. On the other hand, those with low self-efficacy don’t have much confidence they can perform the task successfully This makes them lower their expectations of success; self-efficacy has a negative relationship with procrastination.
Value of task: It is made up of three subsections, task aversiveness (people avoiding unpleasant tasks), need for achievement, and boredom proneness(likelihood of tasks found boring increases).
Distractibility: Distractions will make people prone to setting off tasks and adding delay to the completion of tasks.
Delay of task: Delay of task involves the timing of rewards and punishments, organization, and intention-action gap.
